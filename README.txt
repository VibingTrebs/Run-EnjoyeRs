Run EnjoyeRs is a single-file progressive web app (PWA) that lives on your phone's home screen.
It started as a running coach and is now a HYBRID app — your running and your gym training in
one place, side by side.

  · RUNNING — feed it an AI-generated training plan built from your real run history. It becomes
    your daily coach: today's session, calendar, progress, HR zones, best efforts and share cards.

  · GYM — build your own workout templates and a weekly routine. Your gym days then appear right
    alongside your runs, with a workout logger, rest timer, plate calculator and strength tracking.

  · BOTH — run and lift together. Days with a run AND a workout show a single split card, so your
    week reads as one plan, not two.

When you first open the app it asks what you're here for — Running, Gym, or Both — and sets you up
accordingly. You can change focus any time.

Signing in is OPTIONAL and only needed for the social feed (sharing with friends). Your training
data — plan, runs, workouts — lives on your device. No subscription. No ads.

Run EnjoyeRs uses a plan in JSON format. The easiest way to make one is with Claude AI.

  1. Go to https://claude.ai (a free account is fine) and start a new conversation.

  2. Paste the full contents of  run-enjoyers-plan-prompt.txt  into the message box and send it.
     This is the coach prompt that tells Claude how to build your plan.

  3. Claude will ask you a series of questions, one at a time:
       · Your goal (e.g. 5K, half marathon, improve VO₂ max)
       · Goal race + date, if any
       · Available training days and any day-type preferences
       · Fitness level and current easy pace
       · Whether you use a heart rate monitor
       · Anything else relevant (injuries, travel, etc.)

  4. If you have run files (see Step 4), upload them when Claude asks — it will analyse your
     real paces and patterns to personalise the plan. Last 8–12 weeks is ideal.

  5. Claude shows a plain-English summary and asks you to confirm before generating.

  6. Once confirmed, Claude outputs a block of raw JSON. Copy all of it, paste into a plain-text
     editor, and save as:  my-plan.json

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  TIP: If Claude wraps the JSON in ```json ... ``` fences,   │
  │  delete those lines. The file must start with { and end }   │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

THEN IMPORT IT:

  1. Open Run EnjoyeRs and tap the round profile button (top-right of the Today screen).
  2. Under YOUR PLAN, tap "Update plan" and choose your my-plan.json file.
  3. You'll see "✓ [Plan name] loaded". Tap Today — your first session appears.

The plan is stored locally and survives closing the app or restarting your phone.

Importing past runs lets the app match completed sessions to real data — showing pace, HR,
distance, cadence, power and maps on your runs and share cards, and detecting fitness/awards.

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  EASIEST: CONNECT STRAVA (in-app, no files)                 │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  During onboarding (or later from your profile) tap "Connect Strava", approve access, and the
  app pulls your activities automatically — including route, HR, elevation, pace, cadence, power
  and temperature where recorded. Choose how far back to sync (last month / year / all / custom).

  Later, to backfill or update recent runs: profile → Run history → "Refresh last 7 days from
  Strava".

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  ALTERNATIVE: IMPORT FILES (.fit / .gpx / .tcx / .zip)      │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Tap the History tab → "Import runs" → select multiple files or a ZIP of activities.
  This is also how you get files to upload to Claude in Step 3.

  Where to export files from:
    · STRAVA   — strava.com (desktop) → Settings → My Account → "Download or Delete Your
                 Account" → Request Your Archive. The ZIP has an "activities" folder of .fit/.gpx.
    · GARMIN   — connect.garmin.com → Account Settings → Data Management → Export Your Data.
                 Activities are .fit files under DI_CONNECT/DI-Connect-Fitness.
    · APPLE    — Health app → profile → "Export All Health Data". Or use a tool like HealthFit /
                 WorkOutDoors to export individual workouts as .gpx/.fit.
    · OTHERS   — Polar, Suunto, Wahoo, etc. all support exporting activities as .gpx or .fit.

  Runs that match a planned session (same date ± 1 day, similar distance) are linked
  automatically, with a prompt to confirm.

  STEP 5 — SET UP YOUR GYM   (Gym / Both)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The gym side is built around two things you create: TEMPLATES (a reusable workout) and a
ROUTINE (which template runs on which day of the week). Once set, your gym days show up across
Today, the week strip, the calendar and the full plan.

  1. On the Today screen, tap "Start gym session" (or the onboarding "Set it up now" button).

  2. WORKOUT TEMPLATES — a template is a named workout with a list of exercises (e.g. "Push Day":
     bench, overhead press, dips...). Create one with "+ New template", search the exercise
     library or add your own, and save.

  3. ROUTINE — tap "+ New routine", give it a name, and assign a template to each day of the week
     (e.g. Mon = Push, Wed = Pull, Fri = Legs). Save and make it active. Rest days are just days
     with nothing assigned.

  4. If a gym day lands on the same day as a run, the app handles the clash — keep both (they
     stack into one split card), move the gym day, or move the run.

You can edit templates and routines any time from the same start sheet. Deleting a routine keeps
your templates.

  TODAY  (home)
  ─ Your day at the top: a run hero, a gym hero, or — if you have both — a single split card.
  ─ The week strip shows runs and gym days together; tap a day to preview it.
  ─ The weekly sessions card lists everything for the week, runs and workouts in date order.
    Tap the check on any row to mark it done. Swipe to browse other weeks.
  ─ "View full plan" shows every phase and week with runs and gym interleaved by date.
  ─ The round button (top-right) opens your PROFILE (plan, data, HR, week start, Strava).

  FEED
  ─ The social feed (requires sign-in). Share your weeks and runs, react and comment.

  HISTORY
  ─ All your imported/synced runs in order. Tap one for pace, HR, map, cadence, power,
    best efforts, relative effort and the matched session.

  ZONES
  ─ Your heart-rate zones, derived from your max/resting HR (auto-detected from runs, or set
    them in your profile).

  PROFILE  (round button, top-right of Today)
  ─ YOUR PLAN: update / clear plan, pause training (shifts future sessions forward).
  ─ WEEK START: choose which day your training weeks begin on.
  ─ RUN HISTORY: connect / refresh Strava.
  ─ YOUR DATA: export a backup, restore from backup, download a debug log.

  LOGGING A GYM WORKOUT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  ─ Start a workout from your routine (the gym hero / start sheet), or start a free workout any day.
  ─ For each exercise, log sets with weight (kg) and reps; tick a set to mark it done.
  ─ DURATION / VOLUME / SETS run live across the top.

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  REST TIMER  (top-right of the workout screen)             │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
  ─ Tap REST to pick a length. It starts counting down and becomes your default for future sets.
  ─ While resting, the − / + buttons nudge it by 10 seconds; tap the countdown to change the
    length again. Ticking your next set resets the timer, so to start early just carry on.

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  PLATE CALCULATOR  (barbell icon, top of the workout)      │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
  ─ Enter a target weight and bar weight; it shows exactly which plates to load per side (kg).

  ─ Finish the workout to save it. It counts toward your Strength progress and your weekly total.

  THE WEEKLY WIN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

When you complete the LAST remaining activity in a week — a run or a workout — the app celebrates:
a randomised emoji burst and a summary card of everything you finished that week. From there you
can share it straight to the feed or save it as an image.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  SHARING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  A WEEK   — On Today, tap "Share" on the sessions card to make a summary card for the week, then
             post to the feed or send it anywhere (Messages, WhatsApp, Instagram...).
  A RUN    — Open a run/session, scroll down, tap "Share run". You get a card with your real
             stats; tap a photo to reposition it with pinch-zoom, or switch the photo order.
  THE FEED — Sign in to share with friends and see theirs.

  BACKING UP YOUR DATA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Your plan, runs and workouts are stored locally on your device. They do NOT auto-sync to the
cloud. If you clear your browser data or switch phones, you'll lose progress unless you back up.

  TO BACK UP:  Profile → YOUR DATA → "Export backup". Save the .json somewhere safe (iCloud,
               Google Drive, etc.).
  TO RESTORE:  Profile → YOUR DATA → "Restore backup" → select your file.

Do this regularly, especially before clearing your browser or upgrading your phone.

  TROUBLESHOOTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  Plan shows "No plan loaded"
  → Make sure the JSON starts with { and ends with } and has no extra text. Re-import via
    Profile → Update plan.

  Sessions show on wrong dates
  → Check startDate in your plan JSON (YYYY-MM-DD) and that startDay matches that date's weekday.
    Also check Profile → Week start.

  Progress shows 0 / N sessions
  → Can happen if you imported a new plan over an old one. Profile → Clear plan, then re-import.

  My gym days aren't showing
  → Make sure your routine is ACTIVE and has templates assigned to days. Re-open the start sheet
    (Start gym session) to check the routine.

  Share button does nothing on iPhone
  → Open the app from the home screen (installed PWA), not a Safari tab. The native share sheet
    needs the app running as a PWA.

  Strava connected but no runs
  → Re-open Connect Strava and pick a sync range; then Profile → Run history → "Refresh last 7
    days from Strava" to backfill recent runs.

  Imported run not matching my session
  → The run date must be within ±1 day of the planned session. Open the session to confirm a
    match manually.

  App feels slow after many imports
  → Profile → YOUR DATA → "Export backup", then clear run history and re-import only your most
    recent ~3 months.
