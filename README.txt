━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Run EnjoyeRs is a single-file progressive web app (PWA) that lives on your phone's home screen.
You feed it a training plan — generated by AI using your actual run history — and it becomes your
daily running coach: today's session, calendar view, progress tracking, HR zones, share cards, and more.

No account. No subscription. No ads. Everything stays on your device.

Uploading your past runs lets the AI coach build a plan around your actual fitness —
your real paces, weekly volume, longest runs, and patterns. Skip this if you're starting fresh.

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  FROM STRAVA                                                │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  1. Go to https://www.strava.com on desktop (not the mobile app)
  2. Click your profile photo (top right) → Settings
  3. Scroll to the bottom → click "My Account"
  4. Click "Get Started" under "Download or Delete Your Account"
  5. Select "Request Your Archive" → confirm via the email Strava sends
  6. Wait for the email (usually a few minutes, sometimes up to an hour)
  7. Download the ZIP file — inside you'll find a folder called "activities"
     containing .fit or .gpx files for every run
  8. You can upload the whole ZIP or individual .fit/.gpx files in Step 2

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  FROM GARMIN CONNECT                                        │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  1. Go to https://connect.garmin.com on desktop
  2. Click your name (top right) → Account Settings
  3. Scroll to "Data Management" → "Export Your Data"
  4. Click "Export Account Data" — Garmin will email you a download link
  5. Download and unzip — activities are in the "DI_CONNECT/DI-Connect-Fitness"
     folder as .fit files
  6. Upload these in Step 2

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  FROM APPLE WATCH / HEALTH APP                              │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  1. Open the Health app on your iPhone
  2. Tap your profile photo (top right) → "Export All Health Data"
  3. Tap "Export" → share the ZIP to your Files app or Mac
  4. Unzip and look for workout files (they'll be .xml format)
  5. Alternatively, use a free app like "Health Export" or "WorkOutDoors"
     to export individual workouts as .gpx files

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  FROM OTHER APPS (Polar, Suunto, Wahoo, etc.)               │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Most platforms support exporting individual activities as .gpx or .fit files.
  Look for an "Export" option on any activity detail page on their website.
  .gpx and .fit files are both supported by Run EnjoyeRs.

Run EnjoyeRs uses a plan in JSON format. The easiest way to generate one is with Claude AI.

  1. Go to https://claude.ai (free account works fine)

  2. Start a new conversation

  3. Paste the full contents of the file  run-enjoyers-plan-prompt.txt
     into the message box — this is the coach prompt that tells Claude exactly
     how to build your plan. Send it.

  4. Claude will ask you a series of questions one at a time:
       · Your goal (e.g. 5K, half marathon, improve VO₂ max)
       · Whether you have a goal race + date
       · Your available training days and any day-type preferences
         (e.g. "long run on Sundays", "hard sessions mid-week")
       · Your fitness level and current easy pace
       · Whether you use a heart rate monitor
       · Anything else relevant (injuries, travel, etc.)

  5. If you exported run files in Step 1, upload them when Claude asks.
     Claude will analyse your actual paces and patterns to personalise
     the plan — the more files the better (last 8–12 weeks is ideal).

  6. Claude will show you a plain-English summary of the plan structure
     and ask you to confirm before generating. This is your chance to
     tweak day assignments or session types.

  7. Once you confirm, Claude outputs a block of raw JSON.
     Select all of it, copy it, and paste it into a text editor
     (Notepad on Windows, TextEdit on Mac in plain text mode).
     Save the file as:  my-plan.json

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  TIP: If Claude wraps the JSON in ```json ... ``` fences,   │
  │  just delete those lines — only the { ... } content is      │
  │  needed. The file must start with { and end with }          │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Run EnjoyeRs is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it installs like a native app directly from
the browser. No App Store. No Play Store. Just a bookmark that behaves like an app.

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  ON iPHONE / iPAD (Safari only — must use Safari)           │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  1. Open Safari on your iPhone
  2. Go to:  https://vibingtrebs.github.io/Run-EnjoyeRs/
  3. Wait for the page to fully load
  4. Tap the Share button at the bottom of the screen
     (the square with an arrow pointing up)
  5. Scroll down in the share sheet and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  6. Tap "Add" in the top right corner
  7. The Run EnjoyeRs icon now appears on your home screen
  8. Open it from the home screen — it runs fullscreen with no browser UI

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  ON ANDROID (Chrome)                                        │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  1. Open Chrome on your Android device
  2. Go to:  https://vibingtrebs.github.io/Run-EnjoyeRs/
  3. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top right
  4. Tap "Add to Home screen"
  5. Confirm by tapping "Add"
  6. The icon appears on your home screen — tap it to launch

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  ON DESKTOP (optional)                                      │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Just visit https://vibingtrebs.github.io/Run-EnjoyeRs/ in any browser.
  Chrome and Edge will show an install prompt in the address bar (⊕ icon).
  The app works fully in-browser without installing.

Importing past runs lets the app match your completed sessions to real run data,
showing actual pace, HR and distance on your share cards and in your history.

  1. Tap the "History" tab (bottom left, activity icon)
  2. Tap "Import runs"
  3. Select your .fit, .gpx, or .tcx files — you can select multiple at once,
     or upload a ZIP file containing a folder of activity files
  4. The app will parse each file and add them to your history
  5. Runs that match a planned session (same date ± 1 day, similar distance)
     will be linked automatically — you'll get a prompt to confirm the match

  Going forward, import each run after you complete it to keep your
  history up to date and your share cards accurate.

  ─ Shows your session for today with distance, pace target, HR zone,

  BACKING UP YOUR DATA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Everything is stored in your browser's local storage — it does NOT sync to the cloud
automatically. If you clear your browser data or switch devices, you'll lose your progress
unless you back up first.

  To back up:   You tab → YOUR DATA → "Export backup"
                Save the .json file somewhere safe (iCloud, Google Drive, etc.)

  To restore:   You tab → YOUR DATA → "Restore backup"
                Select your backup file

  Do this regularly, especially before clearing your browser or upgrading your phone.

  Plan shows "No plan loaded"
  → Make sure the JSON file starts with { and ends with } with no extra text.
    Re-import via You tab → Update plan.

  Sessions show on wrong dates
  → Check that startDate in your plan JSON is correct (YYYY-MM-DD format)
    and that startDay matches the day of the week for that date.

  Progress shows 0 / N sessions
  → This can happen if you imported a new plan without clearing the old one first.
    Go to You tab → Clear plan, then re-import your plan file.

  Share button does nothing on iPhone
  → Make sure you opened the app from Safari and installed it to your home screen.
    The native share sheet requires the app to be running as a PWA (not inside a browser tab).

  Imported run not matching my session
  → Check that the run date falls within ±1 day of the planned session date.
    You can also tap a session → scroll to see if a match prompt appears manually.

  App feels slow or laggy after many imports
  → Use You tab → YOUR DATA → "Export backup", then clear run history via
    "Clear run history" and re-import only your most recent 3 months of files.
